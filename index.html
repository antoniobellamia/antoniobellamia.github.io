Center for Space Geodesy Article

The article aims to highlight the significance of the Center for Space Geodesy (CGS) near Matera, Italy. Founded in 1983 and named after Giuseppe Colombo, it pioneers research in space geodesy, utilizing state-of-the-art technologies like the MLRO Laser Telescope and VLBI radio interferometry. Through its work, the CGS contributes crucial data for navigation, meteorology, and climate research, while also fostering innovation and nurturing young talent in space science.
